Day 2 Beijing Tiananmen Square - Forbidden City - Temple of Heaven - Kungfu Show   

  • Breakfast at Hotel     

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • Tiananmen Square Read More

    The Tiananmen - also known as the Gate of Heavenly Peace - is a famous monument in Beijing, China’s capital, intertwined with Chinese history and culture. From the founding of the People’s Republic of China in 1949, a portrait of Mao Zedong has been hung at the Square.

  • Forbidden City Read More

    The Forbidden City is the former place of residence for past emperors, from the Ming Dynasty to the end of the Qing Dynasty. The Outer City or Outer Court was previously used for ceremonial purposes, while the Inner Court was the imperial family’s residence and used for day-to-day state affairs.

  • Lunch

    Enjoy traditional Chinese cuisine at a family style restaurant. A rotating round table presents a multitude of ethnic tastes just one spin away!

  • Temple of Heaven Read More

    Wander the Temple of Heaven grounds, an ornate sanctuary where ancient Chinese emperors came to worship the gods. Light ceremonial incense to further soak in what this magnificent temple offers.

  • Kungfu Show

    Watch an action packed kung fu performance at the Red Theatre. You cannot miss a good martial arts show if you are in China!

Day 3 Beijing The Great Wall of China (Badaling Section) - Ming Tombs - National Stadium   

  • Breakfast at Hotel

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • The Great Wall of China (Badaling Section) Read More

    The Badaling Great Wall - part of the Great Wall of China as the world’s largest man-made project - was continuously built from the 3rd century BC to the 17th century AD. This UNESCO World Heritage Site reflects the defense techniques and technological advancements of ancient China.

  • Lunch

    You are free to explore local restaurants at the foot of the Great Wall for lunch. Our guide will give you a few suggestions.

  • Ming Tombs Read More

    Home to the mausoleums of the Ming Dynasty’s 13 emperors, the Ming Tombs cover a total area of over 120 square kilometres. The Ming Tombs were listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2003. Take a walk along these solemn tombs and feel connected with Chinese history.

  • National Stadium Read More

    On our way back from the historic Ming Tombs, you will see the National Stadium. Also known as the Bird’s Nest. This was the main arena stadium for the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games.

Day 4 Beijing, Beijing-Xi'an The Summer Palace - Hutong   

  • Breakfast at Hotel

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • The Summer Palace Read More

    After breakfast, we’ll take you to the largest royal park in China: the Summer Palace. Boasting hundreds of royal gardens and over 3000 man-made ancient structures, the Summer Palace was once a summer retreat for emperors. Enjoy this royal beauty like the emperors once did!

  • Hutong Read More

    Enjoy a once-in-a-lifetime cycling tour through the Hutongs. Cycle amongst these narrow streets of ancient Beijing and the remnants of old-style local residences. Don't miss the romantic Shichahai Lake! This is your chance of seeing how a true Beijinger lives.

  • Lunch

    Savor tasty Chinese cuisine to refuel after an adventurous morning. It's your chance to perfect the skill of rotating the round table!

  • Depart for Xi'an Read More

    After lunch, our guide would send you off to airport for your departure to Xi'an, the starting point of the famous Silk Road. Once there, try some local Xi’an cuisine, and get rested for another eventful day!

Day 5 Xi'an Terracotta Warriors - Small Wild Goose Pagoda   

  • Breakfast at Hotel

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • Terracotta Warriors Read More

    The Eighth Wonder of the world. The moment you've been waiting for! Are you excited? The eternal warriors and horses housed in the Qin Shi Huang Mausoleum are listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987 for their sensational and outstanding archaeological value.

  • Lunch

    This is no ordinary lunch! Spend this time in the home of a local resident and learn to cook an authentic Chinese dish. Truly immerse yourself in the local lifestyle. The culture is just a taste away, excuse the pun!

  • Small Wild Goose Pagoda Read More

    One of the two legendary pagodas of Xi'an, this pagoda was built over 1,300 years ago during the Tang dynasty. Also a UNESCO World Heritage site, come see where ancient Buddhist monks used to transcribe the first scriptures.

  • Dinner

    The Famous Dumpling Feast. Enjoy a music and dance performance of the renowned Tang dynasty while chowing down savory traditional Xi'an dumplings.

Day 6 Xi'an, Xi'an-Shanghai Xi'an City Wall - Shaanxi History Museum   

  • Breakfast at Hotel

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • Xi'an City Wall Read More

    Today we will see some ancient Chinese Military might. Also known as the Fortification of Xi’an, the wall is one of the best preserved city walls in China, completed in the Ming Dynasty after Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang expanded the wall originally built in the Tang Dynasty.

  • Shaanxi History Museum Read More

    The museum showcases the history and development of Chinese culture by arranging artifacts chronologically across the three exhibition halls. Some world famous exhibits include the Treasures of the Great Tang Dynasty and the Dynasty Murals.

  • Lunch

    Shaanxi cuisine is full of lamb, handmade noodles and exquisite spices. Lunch is on us!

  • Depart for Shanghai Read More

    Time to depart to Shanghai. Our expert guide will be with you every step of the way until you get on your plane safely and on time!

Day 7 Shanghai Shanghai Museum - Yu Garden - The Bund - Huangpu River Cruise - Jin Mao Tower   

  • Breakfast at Hotel

    Get ready for a big day of adventure with a hearty breakfast!

  • Shanghai Museum Read More

    The Shanghai Museum features over 14,000 pieces of ancient Chinese art - including bronze, furnitures, jade, paintings - across 11 galleries and 3 temporary special exhibition halls.

  • Yu Garden Read More

    Nestled in the heart of the bustling city, the Yu Garden was a private garden for Chinese royalty during the Ming Dynasty. First constructed in 1559, the garden exemplifies the architectural landscape of ancient China.

  • Lunch

    Luxury Farewell Lunch - China will miss you! Enjoy traditional Shanghainese and Cantonese cuisine in a top-ranked Shanghai restaurant.

  • The Bund Read More

    Jetbay looks forward to introducing to you one of the most famous destinations in Shanghai. Featuring buildings of 26 architectural styles, sculptures of legendary leaders, as well as night views of Pudong and the Huangpu River, the Bund is one of the most recognized locations in the world.

  • Huangpu River Cruise Read More

    After viewing the Bund on land, its time to sail it! The River Cruise offers stunning views of glittering skyscrapers, including the Jin Mao and Oriental Pearl Towers and a peek into the Pudong New Area, all the while sailing alongside the Bund on the west bank.

  • Jin Mao Tower Read More

    There's no better way to see a bird's-eye view of Shanghai than seeing from the Jin Mao Tower observation deck! This 88-story building is the 3rd tallest building in mainland China and home to the Grand Hyatt, one of Shanghai's world class five star hotels.
